01

Overview & Intent

DP2 is an applied AR game concept for autistic children (5–10 yrs), many with DCD. It blends a learning game with light data collection for gentle progress tracking. Not a medical device.

02

Research Synthesis (Questions → Answers → Inferences)

Questions
  • Which motor outcomes should we scaffold first?
  • How do color, sound, and character style affect attention?
  • Which signals show progress without burden?
  • What session format sustains participation?
Answers
  • Big, colorful, predictable targets + gentle sounds help focus.
  • Familiar animation language increases willingness to try.
  • 10–15 minute routines with breaks are effective.
Inferences
  • Low cognitive load; repeatable structures; gradual scaling (~10%).
  • Avatar mirroring improves clarity and agency.
  • Track accuracy, achievable height/distance, reaction/movement time, L/R balance.

03

Target Users & Constraints

Users
  • Autistic kids (~5–10); subset with DCD.
  • Caregivers & clinicians (OT/SLP).
Constraints
  • Variable attention; sensory sensitivities.
  • Limited safe space; household distractions.
  • Need predictable transitions & clear affordances.
04

Learning Objectives

  • Reach height & accuracy; graded force.
  • Stepping & balance; bilateral coordination; crossing midline.
  • Hand–eye coordination; timing & rhythm.
  • Self‑regulation via co‑regulation breaks.
05

Design Principles

  • Predictable rhythm: same loop each session.
  • Gentle scaffolding: 10% steps; one rule at a time.
  • Positive reinforcement: specific praise; tokens; no buzzers.
  • Agency: avatar mirrors child; meaningful choices.
  • Sensory‑smart: saturated‑soft colors; quiet SFX; Quiet Mode.
  • Safety‑first: short‑step/in‑place; AR bounds.
  • Privacy‑by‑default: local storage; consent for export.
06

Engagement Model

  • Mascot guide introduces “today’s quest”.
  • Player‑avatar mirrors movement (“pick & place” appears on screen).
  • Token board rewards every 1–2 rounds.
  • Calm Cave break keeps arousal in green zone.
07

Sensory Direction

Color
  • Saturated but gentle palette; high‑contrast toggle.
  • Large shapes; minimal clutter.
Sound
  • Warm VO; soft success tones; no harsh fails.
  • Volume presets; quick Quiet Mode.
Character
  • Friendly, expressive animation style (“Disney‑like” language; no licensed IP).
  • Mascot + avatar mapped to movement.
08

Data & Measurement Strategy

Signals
  • Accuracy, height/distance achieved.
  • Reaction/movement time (device‑dependent).
  • L/R balance, midline crosses.
  • Tolerability: session length, breaks.
Use
  • Trendlines for adaptation & caregiver insight.
  • No diagnosis; optional anonymized CSV export.
  • Consent prompts; clear data dictionary.
Compliance: Not a medical device.
09

Safety, Accessibility & Ethics

  • AR bounds; out‑of‑bounds nudge.
  • In‑place/short steps; no sprints.
  • Pause/skip/end; difficulty sliders.
  • Privacy by default; deletion & export controls.
10

Narrative Frame & Theme

A gentle world where a mascot invites the child to bring color & calm to the room. The avatar mirrors the child’s actions so each movement is validated on screen.

11

Concept KPIs

  • ≥10 min average session tolerance.
  • Upward trend in achievable height/distance.
  • Balanced L/R reps; reduced breaks over time.
  • Positive caregiver/therapist ratings.
12

Pilot Plan

  • N=6–10 kids; 2–3 sessions.
  • Track success %, tolerability, qualitative feedback.
  • Tune sensory presets & pacing.
13

Risks & Mitigations

Overstimulation
  • Quiet mode; dim palette; fewer particles.
Tracking drift
  • Large colliders; re‑anchors; reset.
Space limits
  • In‑place activities; short steps.
Licensing
  • Original characters; no 3rd‑party IP.
